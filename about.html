About the Project

Built to make nature count on every balance sheet

EcoValue India is an open-source ecosystem services valuation engine built for Indian policymakers, planners, and communities — powered by peer-reviewed Indian research, RAG-enhanced AI, and a FastAPI backend.

"India loses an estimated ₹7 lakh crore annually from ecosystem degradation — simply because those services never appear on balance sheets."
— Pavan Sukhdev · TEEB India · 2008
Methodology

How we calculate ecosystem value

01
Replacement Cost Method
For water filtration and flood control: we calculate the cost of replacing the ecosystem service with engineered infrastructure. A 1 ha wetland providing flood buffering for 7 ha downstream is valued at the avoided damage cost.
02
Social Cost of Carbon
Carbon sequestration is valued using India's social cost of carbon (₹3,500/tonne) or voluntary market prices (₹2,200/tonne). Rates sourced from MoEFCC NATCOM and FSI ISFR 2023 carbon stock data.
03
Willingness-to-Pay Studies
Biodiversity and recreation values use WTP surveys from ATREE, TERI, and Hussain & Badola (2010) across Indian communities. Regional variation is captured through 9 regional multipliers.
04
Market Price Method
Fisheries, timber, and NWFP services use prevailing market prices from CMFRI, FSI, and state fisheries departments. Pollination is valued at crop production value × dependency ratio (TERI 2016).
AI Architecture

RAG-grounded policy narratives

Our AI narratives don't hallucinate numbers. Before generating any policy brief, the system retrieves the most relevant documents from a 91-document knowledge base built directly from your coefficient database, real Indian case studies, and policy frameworks.

The result: policy briefs that automatically cite Chilika Lake precedents, Sundarbans cyclone data, and India NDC commitments — grounded in the same data used by TERI and MoEFCC.

Case Studies in RAG Index

Real Indian decisions that inform our AI

Inland Wetland · Odisha
Chilika Lake: Conservation vs Prawn Aquaculture
India's largest coastal lagoon. 200,000 fisher families. Commercial aquaculture offered ₹25,000/ha lease. TERI valuation showed ecosystem value at 3.2× aquaculture revenue over 10 years.
✅ Outcome: Conservation chosen after full ecosystem accounting
Tropical Moist Forest · Western Ghats
Western Ghats: Forest vs Iron Ore Mining
Forest ecosystem value ₹8.7L/ha/yr vs mining royalty ₹4.5L/ha/yr. 10-year NPV forest: ₹58L/ha. Mining: ₹30L/ha. Net loss if mined: ₹28L/ha over decade.
✅ Outcome: Mining moratorium upheld by Supreme Court 2012
Mangroves · West Bengal
Sundarbans: Mangrove vs Industrial Port
800 ha mangrove proposed for port expansion. Annual ecosystem loss ₹720 crore vs port revenue ₹200 crore. Cyclone Amphan 2020: mangroves reduced impact by 40% saving ₹40,000 crore in damage.
⚠ Outcome: Conservation strongly recommended by WII 2021
Urban Green · Karnataka
Bengaluru: Urban Lake vs IT Park
45 ha urban lake vs IT park. Apparent gap: ₹172 Cr over 10 years. When stormwater, AC energy, health, and flood costs added: advantage drops to just ₹26 Cr. 2022 floods cost ₹400 crore.
📊 Outcome: True cost accounting closes the apparent development advantage
